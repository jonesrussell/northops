## Shared Principles

- **Build, then talk.** Ship working software before writing about it. Results are the only credible marketing.
- **Direct communication.** Say what you mean. No hedging, no corporate filler, no performative language.
- **Judgement over process.** Senior engineers apply judgement to each situation. Process is a tool, not a substitute for thinking.
- **Respect the system.** Understand the existing codebase, the constraints, and the people who built it before proposing changes.
- **Scope tight, deliver fast.** Small, well-defined engagements with explicit deliverables. Prove value before asking for more commitment.
- **No overhead by default.** Coordination layers exist to serve the work, not the other way around. Remove them unless they're genuinely needed.
- **Own the outcome.** The engineers who scope the work are the engineers who deliver it. No handoffs, no blame-shifting, no "that's not my area."

## How We Complement Each Other

- **Full-stack coverage:** Russell covers backend (Laravel, Drupal, Go, Rust) and infrastructure; the second founder covers frontend (React, TypeScript) and AI integration — no handoff gap between layers.
- **Breadth without dilution:** Each founder has deep expertise in their domain rather than shallow familiarity across everything. Clients get two specialists, not two generalists.
- **Dual DevOps capability:** Both founders build CI/CD pipelines and deploy automation, meaning DevOps work can be delivered by either founder independently — no single point of failure.
- **Architecture and planning:** Russell brings platform architecture and technical audit experience; the second founder brings technical planning and documentation. Together, they scope engagements with precision.
- **AI as a team capability:** The second founder leads AI Engineering while Russell provides the backend infrastructure (APIs, pipelines, data flow) that AI features depend on. AI work ships as a complete system, not an isolated prototype.
