# Issue #2: Define Target Client Profiles

**Title:** Define target client profiles — validate 3 segments against service lines

**Labels:** strategy

**Milestone:** M1: Company Definition & Positioning

## Body

## Description

Using the 4 service lines defined in `strategy/m1-service-offerings.md` (Web Application Engineering, Content & Data Pipelines, DevOps & CI/CD, AI Engineering), validate which client segments will buy first.

## Proposed Client Segments (3)

### Segment 1: Digital Agencies with Overflow

**Hypothesis:** Mid-sized digital agencies (10–50 people) regularly hit capacity limits on senior backend or DevOps work and will pay NorthOps rates to avoid missing deadlines.

- **Service lines they buy:** Web Application Engineering, DevOps & CI/CD
- **Why they pay:** They already have the client and the deadline — they need the engineer. Senior-only matters because agency overflow work is usually the hardest part of the project.
- **How to validate:** Reach out to 5 agencies Russell and Luc have worked with or near. Ask: "When was the last time you turned down work or missed a deadline because you didn't have a senior backend/DevOps person available?"

### Segment 2: Startups with a Working Product and No DevOps

**Hypothesis:** Seed-to-Series-A startups (2–15 people) that have a working product but deploy manually and have no CI/CD will pay for a short sprint to set up their pipeline.

- **Service lines they buy:** DevOps & CI/CD, Web Application Engineering
- **Why they pay:** DevOps pain is acute and visible (broken deploys, manual steps, fear of pushing to prod). The MVP is small and fast (3 days). Low commitment to start.
- **How to validate:** Reach out to 5 startups in the local network or on LinkedIn. Ask: "How do you deploy right now? What breaks?"

### Segment 3: SMBs Adding AI to an Existing Product

**Hypothesis:** Small-to-medium businesses with an existing web product will pay a premium for a senior engineer to integrate a specific AI feature (search, summarization, automation) rather than hiring or experimenting internally.

- **Service lines they buy:** AI Engineering, Content & Data Pipelines
- **Why they pay:** AI is high-demand but high-risk. They want someone who has shipped it before, not someone learning on their dime. The premium rate is justified by reduced risk.
- **How to validate:** Reach out to 5 product companies (SaaS, content platforms, internal tools). Ask: "Is there a specific AI feature your users or team have asked for that you haven't built yet? What stopped you?"

## Validation Plan

1. **Write outreach scripts** — One per segment (3 total). Each script is a 3-sentence email or LinkedIn message that asks the validation question above. No pitch, just discovery.
2. **Identify 5 target prospects per segment** — 15 total. Prioritize warm contacts (former clients, colleagues, local network). Fill remaining slots with targeted LinkedIn search.
3. **Run a 1-week discovery sprint** — Send all outreach in week 1. Track responses. Score each segment on: response rate, expressed pain level, willingness to discuss budget. Report findings in a short memo and decide which 1–2 segments to pursue first.

## Acceptance Criteria
- 3 client segments defined with testable hypotheses
- 5 target prospects identified per segment (15 total)
- Outreach scripts written and ready to send
- 1-week discovery sprint scheduled with clear success metrics
