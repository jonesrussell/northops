## Segment 1: Agency Overflow

### Segment name
Digital agencies needing senior engineers for overflow projects

### One-line problem statement
Small-to-mid agencies (5–50 people) lose revenue when they hit capacity on senior backend, frontend, or DevOps work and can't staff it fast enough.

### Why NorthOps fits
Russell covers Laravel, Drupal, and infrastructure — the backend stacks agencies use most. Luc covers React, Rails, and TypeScript — the frontend and API layers. Both ship independently with no ramp-up time, so agencies get senior capacity on demand without onboarding overhead.

### Top 3 testable value propositions
- No ramp-up: senior engineers who have shipped production code in your stack before
- No overhead: no PMs, no account managers — direct access to the engineers doing the work
- Flexible engagement: per-project or weekly retainer, scale up or down as backlog changes

### Quick validation experiment
1. **Outreach script:** See `sales/outreach-agency-overflow.md` — a 30–60 word message asking about recent capacity gaps
2. **5 prospect archetypes to contact:** (a) agency Russell has subcontracted for before, (b) agency Luc has worked with, (c) local Northern Ontario agency, (d) Toronto/Montreal mid-size digital agency found on Clutch, (e) agency that recently posted a senior developer job listing
3. **1-week discovery sprint:** Send 5 outreach messages on day 1. Follow up on day 3. Book discovery calls by day 5. Document pain points, budgets, and engagement preferences by day 7.

### Success criteria
Validated in 7 days if: at least 2 discovery calls completed AND at least 1 prospect expresses willingness to send a paid trial project.

### Suggested MVP offer
2-week embedded senior dev sprint (one founder, full-time on the agency's project): **CAD $12,000–$14,400**
`Assumption: 10 dev days @ CAD $1,200–$1,440/day = CAD $12,000–$14,400`

## Segment 2: Startups Without DevOps

### Segment name
Early-stage startups that need production-ready infrastructure and CI/CD

### One-line problem statement
Seed-to-Series-A startups (2–15 people) with a working product deploy manually, have no CI/CD, and lose engineering hours to broken deploys and environment drift.

### Why NorthOps fits
Russell builds CI/CD pipelines (GitHub Actions, Deployer), provisions servers (Caddy, Nginx, systemd), and writes Go/Rust CLI tools for automation. Luc sets up Docker, IaC, and GitHub Actions pipelines. Either founder can deliver the full DevOps MVP independently in 3 days.

### Top 3 testable value propositions
- Fast ROI: a working CI/CD pipeline in 3 days — your team ships faster from week 1
- No hire needed: get production-grade infra without adding a full-time DevOps engineer to payroll
- Small commitment: fixed-price sprint, not an ongoing retainer — try it and see the results

### Quick validation experiment
1. **Outreach script:** See `sales/outreach-startups-no-devops.md` — a 30–60 word message asking how they deploy today
2. **5 prospect archetypes to contact:** (a) startup founder in personal network, (b) YC/Techstars company with <15 employees from LinkedIn, (c) local Northern Ontario tech startup, (d) startup that recently raised seed funding (Crunchbase), (e) dev tool startup posting about deploy issues on Twitter/X
3. **1-week discovery sprint:** Send 5 outreach messages on day 1. Follow up on day 3. Book discovery calls by day 5. On calls, ask: "How do you deploy? What breaks? How many hours per week does deploy take?" Document by day 7.

### Success criteria
Validated in 7 days if: at least 2 discovery calls completed AND at least 1 prospect requests a scoping proposal for a CI/CD sprint.

### Suggested MVP offer
3-day DevOps sprint (CI/CD pipeline + server provisioning for one project): **CAD $3,600–$4,320**
`Assumption: 3 dev days @ CAD $1,200–$1,440/day = CAD $3,600–$4,320`

## Segment 3: SMBs Adding AI

### Segment name
Small/medium businesses exploring LLM, RAG, or AI automation features

### One-line problem statement
SMBs with an existing web product want to add a specific AI capability (search, summarization, automation) but lack the in-house expertise to build it safely for production.

### Why NorthOps fits
Luc builds production AI integrations: LLM workflows, embeddings, RAG pipelines, and Claude API agent workflows. He also handles the Python/TypeScript backend that AI features run on. Russell provides the platform and pipeline infrastructure AI features depend on (APIs, data ingestion, deploy automation).

### Top 3 testable value propositions
- Production-grade, not prototype: AI features built by a senior engineer, tested, and deployed — not a Jupyter notebook handed off
- Specific scope: one defined AI feature (RAG search, summarization, agent workflow) with a fixed price and timeline
- No ML team needed: add AI to your existing product without hiring specialists or buying a platform

### Quick validation experiment
1. **Outreach script:** See `sales/outreach-smbs-adding-ai.md` — a 30–60 word message asking about AI features their users have requested
2. **5 prospect archetypes to contact:** (a) SaaS company with a content-heavy product (knowledge base, docs), (b) internal tools company that processes large document volumes, (c) e-commerce company wanting product search or recommendations, (d) professional services firm exploring automation, (e) company that posted about AI interest on LinkedIn
3. **1-week discovery sprint:** Send 5 outreach messages on day 1. Follow up on day 3. Book discovery calls by day 5. On calls, ask: "What specific AI feature have your users or team asked for? What stopped you from building it?" Document by day 7.

### Success criteria
Validated in 7 days if: at least 2 discovery calls completed AND at least 1 prospect has a concrete AI use case with budget willingness.
