# NorthOps — Visual Identity Direction

> How NorthOps should look and feel across web, docs, and sales assets.

---

## How NorthOps Should Look

Minimal, modern, technical, clean. The design should feel like it was built by engineers — measured, intentional, and confident without being loud. Every visual element serves a purpose: communication, hierarchy, or orientation. Nothing decorative.

The overall aesthetic is quiet competence. A clean workshop, not a showroom.

---

## Colour Direction

- **Text:** Deep, near-black (slate/charcoal) — not pure black
- **Background:** Off-white or very light warm grey — not pure white
- **Accent:** A single muted cool tone (blue-grey, forest green, or steel blue) — used sparingly for links, buttons, and interactive elements only
- **Semantic:** Standard muted green/red/amber for success/error/warning states

The accent colour is a highlight, not a theme. If more than 10% of a page is the accent colour, it's overused.

---

## Typography Direction

- **Headings:** Clean geometric or grotesque sans-serif. Medium to semi-bold weight. Authoritative, not decorative.
- **Body:** Highly legible sans-serif optimized for screen reading. Regular weight, generous x-height. Readable at 16px.
- **Code:** Well-designed monospace for code blocks, stack names, CLI commands.
- **Three levels max** in any section: heading, subheading, body. If you need a fourth, restructure.
- **Generous line height:** Body at 1.5–1.7, headings at 1.2–1.3.

---

## Layout & Structure

- Content-first layout with generous whitespace
- Consistent spacing scale with predictable increments
- Left-align everything (centered text only for hero headlines)
- One idea per section — if a section has a description AND a table AND a CTA, split it
- Low-to-medium density: fewer elements, more space, more authority

---

## Imagery

**Use:**
- Text-based pipeline and architecture diagrams (clean, minimal)
- Short syntax-highlighted code snippets showing real config or deploy scripts
- Terminal/CLI screenshots (cropped tightly, no window chrome)
- Documentary-style photography if needed (real workspaces, Northern Ontario)

**Avoid:**
- Stock photography of any kind
- Abstract geometric or isometric tech illustrations
- AI-generated art
- Decorative icons or blob shapes
- Full-bleed background images

---

## What We Avoid

- Gradient backgrounds
- Animated hero sections or parallax scrolling
- Rounded cards with drop shadows
- Dark mode as the only option
- Emoji in professional copy
- Testimonial carousels
- Excessive iconography or brand colour overuse
- Anything that signals "startup landing page" or "agency portfolio"

NorthOps should look like what it is: a senior engineering company that builds things.
