## The NorthOps Story

NorthOps started with a straightforward observation: most teams that need senior engineering work done have bad options. Agencies are expensive and layered — by the time the work reaches an engineer, it has passed through account managers, project coordinators, and scoping committees. Freelancers are fast but unreliable — narrow stack coverage, unpredictable availability, and no one to call when things go wrong. Internal hires solve the quality problem but take months to recruit and commit the company to a full-time salary for what might be a two-week project.

Russell and Luc built NorthOps to be the option that doesn't exist yet: a two-person senior engineering company that delivers across the full stack — backend, frontend, infrastructure, CI/CD, and AI — with no layers between the client and the work. Russell brings deep expertise in PHP, Laravel, Drupal, Go, Rust, content pipelines, and platform architecture. Luc brings expertise in TypeScript, React, Rails, Python, AI integration, and technical planning. Together, they cover the stack without handoff gaps and without coordination overhead.

The company is based in Northern Ontario. The name reflects the geography and the approach — direct, grounded, built for conditions that don't tolerate waste. NorthOps doesn't sell hours, resources, or partnerships. It sells working software, delivered by the engineers who scoped it, on the timeline they committed to.

The model is simple by design. Fixed-price MVPs with explicit deliverables. A simple SOW and e-transfer to start. No procurement portals, no vendor questionnaires, no six-week onboarding processes. The first paid engagement can close in days. Retainers and longer engagements are available but never required — clients try the work before committing to anything more.

NorthOps is senior-only because the work demands it. Architecture decisions, production deploys, legacy modernization, AI integration — these are not tasks you hand to a junior and review later. Every hour of NorthOps time is a senior engineer applying judgement to a real problem. That's the product.

## Why We Exist

- **Agencies add overhead.** Clients pay for project managers, account managers, and coordination layers — not just the engineering. NorthOps removes the layers so clients pay only for the work.
- **Freelancers are single points of failure.** One person, one stack, unpredictable availability. NorthOps is two senior engineers covering the full stack with a real company behind the work.
- **Hiring is slow and expensive.** Recruiting a senior engineer takes months and costs six figures. NorthOps delivers senior engineering in days, on demand, without the commitment.
- **AI capability is scarce.** Most agencies and freelancers cannot deliver production-grade AI integration. NorthOps offers it as a core service line, not an experiment.
- **Small projects fall through the cracks.** A 3-day CI/CD setup or a 5-day feature build isn't worth an agency's attention. NorthOps is built for exactly this size of work.

## How We Work

NorthOps works the way senior engineers prefer to work: with clear scope, direct communication, and fast feedback loops.

**Scoping is engineering, not sales.** The engineers who will do the work are the ones who scope it. There is no handoff between a sales team and a delivery team. The person on the discovery call is the person writing the code.

**Engagements are small and fast.** Fixed-price MVPs in 3–10 days. Short sprints for exploratory work. Retainers for ongoing capacity. Every engagement has explicit deliverables, a fixed timeline, and clear acceptance criteria.

**Starting is frictionless.** The first engagement uses a simple 1–2 page SOW. Payment is 50% upfront via e-transfer, 50% on delivery. No MSA required until the third engagement or any single engagement exceeding CAD $15,000.

**Communication is direct.** No status meetings, no weekly reports, no Gantt charts. Clients get direct access to the engineer doing the work. Updates happen in the repo, in Slack, or in a short daily message — whatever the client prefers.

## What We Believe

1. **Senior engineers should do senior work.** Architecture, production systems, and critical integrations require judgement that comes from experience. Don't delegate these to juniors and call it mentoring.

2. **Speed comes from clarity, not shortcuts.** Fast delivery means tight scope, explicit deliverables, and engineers who don't need to be taught the stack. It doesn't mean cutting corners or skipping tests.

3. **Overhead is a cost, not a value-add.** Every person between the client and the engineer adds cost and latency. Remove them unless they're genuinely needed.

4. **The first engagement should be easy.** If it takes six weeks and a procurement process to start working together, something is wrong. Lower the barrier, prove the value, then formalize.

5. **Say what you do, do what you say.** Every claim should be backed by a deliverable, a price, or a timeline. No aspirational language, no future promises. If we haven't done it, we don't say we do it.

6. **AI is engineering, not magic.** LLM integration, RAG pipelines, and agent workflows are engineering problems with engineering solutions. They require the same rigour as any other production system.

7. **Northern means grounded.** The work comes first. The marketing comes after. Build the thing, then talk about it — not the other way around.

Two engineers covering backend, frontend, infrastructure, CI/CD, and AI integration means clients don't need to coordinate between multiple vendors. One team, one point of contact, one codebase.

AI Engineering is not an add-on or an experiment. It is a defined service line with production-grade delivery, explicit pricing, and clear scope. This positions NorthOps ahead of agencies and freelancers who treat AI as speculative.
